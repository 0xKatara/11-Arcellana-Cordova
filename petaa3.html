INTRAMUROS


The historic walled city located in the heart of Manila. You can explore ancient fortifications like Fort Santiago and well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture.
Masungi Georeserve
A conservation area tucked in the rainforest of Rizal. It features a thrilling network of hanging bridges, rope courses, and stunning karst pinnacles, located about 1.5 to 2 hours east of Metro Manila.
Mayon Volcano
Mayon is a perfect stratovolcano in Albay, Philippines, famous for its symmetrical cone. Active and majestic, its stunning yet dangerous beauty draws tourists worldwide, who come to witness its frequent.
Mount Pinatubo
Located along the borders of Zambales, Tarlac, and Pampanga. You can take a 4x4 off-road ride followed by a trek to see the mesmerizing aquamarine crater lake formed by its 1991 eruption.
Rizal Park
Rizal Park, also known as Luneta Park, is a historic 58-hectare urban park in Ermita, Manila, Philippines. Situated along Manila Bay and adjacent to the walled city of Intramuros, It is one of the largest parks in Asia.

VISAYAS:


Boracay
Boracay is a world-renowned resort island in Aklan, Philippines, famous for its 4-kilometer powdery white-sand White Beach, vibrant nightlife, and crystal-clear turquoise waters. Located 315 kilometers south of Manila.
Chocolate Hills
The Chocolate Hills are a famous geological formation in Bohol, Philippines. They consist of over 1,260 to 1,776 conical mounds that are covered in green grass, which turns a striking chocolate-brown during the dry season.
Kawasan Falls
Kawasan Falls, located in Badian, Cebu, is a famous multi-tiered waterfall known for its striking, crystal-clear turquoise waters. The entrance fee is ₱200, which grants access to the main pool. Visitors typically spend the day swimming.
Kalanggaman Island
Kalanggaman Island is a postcard-perfect tropical paradise in Palompon, Leyte, celebrated globally for its pristine powdery white sands and iconic, shifting sandbars. Measuring about 750 meters in length, famous for its crystalline waters.
Panglao Island
Panglao Island, located in Bohol, Philippines, is a premier tropical destination famous for powdery white-sand beaches, vibrant marine life, and world-class diving. Connected to Bohol's mainland by two bridges.

MINDANAO:


Siargao Island
Siargao is a teardrop-shaped island in Surigao del Norte, Philippines, globally renowned as the country's surfing capital. Located about 800 kilometers southeast of Manila, it is a two-hour flight away. The primary hub is General Luna, famous for its lively "Tourism Road"
Camiguin
Camiguin is an island province in Northern Mindanao, Philippines, famous as the "Island Born of Fire" due to its seven volcanoes. Located off the northern coast of Mindanao, this pear-shaped gem offers a mix of volcanic landscapes, hot/cold springs, jungle waterfalls.
Hinatuan River
The Hinatuan Enchanted River is a deep, crystal-clear spring river in Surigao del Sur, Philippines. Famed for its sapphire- and jade-colored waters that seemingly appear from nowhere, Located in Talisay, Hinatuan
Davao City
Davao City is the economic, cultural, and financial hub of Mindanao, and the largest city in the Philippines by land area. Located in the southeastern part of the island, it is famously known as the "Durian Capital of the Philippines" and serves as the gateway to Mount Apo.
Tinuy-an Falls
Tinuy-an Falls in Bislig City, Surigao del Sur, is a majestic three-tiered waterfall often dubbed the "Niagara Falls of the Philippines." Spanning 95 meters wide and 55 meters high, it is celebrated for its breathtaking curtain-like cascade and pristine natural pools.
